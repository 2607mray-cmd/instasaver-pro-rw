## Copyright Law and Fair Use

Now let's discuss the legal framework that governs content ownership and usage.

### What is Copyright?

Copyright is a form of intellectual property protection that gives creators exclusive rights to their work. When someone creates and posts an Instagram video, they automatically own the copyright to that content.

**Copyright Protects:**
- Original videos and photos
- Music and audio
- Text and captions
- Creative edits and effects

**Copyright Gives Creators the Right To:**
- Reproduce the work
- Distribute copies
- Create derivative works
- Display the work publicly
- License or sell rights

### The Fair Use Doctrine

Fair use is a legal doctrine that allows limited use of copyrighted material without permission under certain circumstances.

**The Four Fair Use Factors:**

1. **Purpose and Character of Use**
   - Educational, commentary, criticism, news reporting = More likely fair use
   - Commercial use = Less likely fair use
   - Transformative use (adding new meaning) = More likely fair use

2. **Nature of the Copyrighted Work**
   - Factual content = More likely fair use
   - Creative content = Less likely fair use

3. **Amount and Substantiality**
   - Using small portions = More likely fair use
   - Using entire work = Less likely fair use

4. **Effect on Market Value**
   - Doesn't harm creator's income = More likely fair use
   - Competes with original = Less likely fair use

**Source:** [U.S. Copyright Office - Fair Use](https://www.copyright.gov/fair-use/)
